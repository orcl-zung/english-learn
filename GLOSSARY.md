# English Pronunciation Glossary

Terms for connected speech & rhythm. Grammar terms will be added in Phase 2.

## Connected speech

**Connected speech (连读 / 连音):**
The way words join in natural, fluent speech so a sentence flows as one stream of sound rather than isolated words.
_Avoid:_ "fast speech", "slurring"

**Linking (连读):**
Connecting the final sound of one word to the first sound of the next so there is no gap between them.
_Avoid:_ blending (reserve for when two sounds merge into one)

**Consonant-to-vowel linking:**
When a word ends in a consonant sound and the next begins with a vowel sound, the consonant hitches a ride onto the vowel: *an apple* → *a-napple*.

**Elision (吞音):**
A sound disappears in fluent speech, usually /t/ or /d/ caught between consonants: *next day* → *ne(k)s day*.

**Schwa /ə/:**
The reduced, relaxed vowel — the most common vowel in English. It is why unstressed syllables sound so short and vague: *about*, the second syllable of *photograph*.

**Thought group (意群):**
A short stretch of speech said as one unit, with a tiny pause at each end. Linking happens *inside* a thought group, never across its pauses.

Phase 1 · Speaking Lesson 0003 · ~9 min

Vowel → Vowel Linking （元音 + 元音：/w/ /j/ /r/）

You've heard these in every daily — "go-w-away", "he-y-is", "thei·r·own". Now here's the system behind them.

Today's win: When two vowels collide, know instantly which glide slides in to connect them — /w/, /j/, or (British) /r/ — based on how the first vowel ends.

The idea （嘴型决定一切）

前两课讲辅音怎么连。这一课：词 1 以元音结尾、词 2 也以元音开头时怎么办？

嘴没法干净地从一个元音跳到另一个（尤其嘴型要变的时候），所以英语会偷偷在中间塞一个滑音（glide）。塞哪个，取决于第一个元音结束时你嘴的形状：

第一个元音怎么收尾塞什么触发元音例子
嘴圆 / 收圆/w//uː ʊ əʊ aʊ/go away, do it, how are
嘴角展开/j//iː ɪ eɪ aɪ ɔɪ/he is, I am, see it
(英音) 以 /ə/ /ɔː/ 收尾/r/历史上带 r 的词far away, there is, more of

记忆口诀：圆→/w/，咧→/j/，英音余音→/r/。

① /w/ — after round-lip vowels （圆唇收尾）

The first vowel ends with your lips rounded, so they naturally glide into a /w/ to reach the next vowel.

go·w·away
go away  ·  /ɡəʊ əˈweɪ/ → /ɡəʊ·w·əˈweɪ/
do·w·it
do it  ·  /duː ɪt/ → /duː·w·ɪt/

More:

  • how are — /aʊ/ 圆唇 → /w/
  • too often — /uː/ → /w/
  • now and then — /aʊ/ → /w/

② /j/ — after spread vowels （嘴角展开收尾）

The first vowel ends with lips spread (smile-ish), gliding into a /j/ (a quick "y" sound).

he·j·is
he is  ·  /hiː ɪz/ → /hiː·j·ɪz/
I·j·am
I am  ·  /aɪ æm/ → /aɪ·j·æm/

More:

  • see it — /iː/ → /j/
  • they are — /eɪ/ → /j/
  • the end — 元音前 the 读 /ði/，再塞 /j/

③ linking /r/ — the British signature （英音招牌）

英音里词尾的 r 通常不发音（car /kɑː/、four /fɔː/）。但当后面紧跟一个元音时，这个 r 会复活——这就是 linking /r/。

far·r·away
far away  ·  /fɑː əˈweɪ/ → /fɑː·r·əˈweɪ/
there·r·is
there is  ·  /ðeə ɪz/ → /ðeə·r·ɪz/

More:

  • more of
  • your own
  • mother and father
进阶 · intrusive /r/（侵入式 r）：连拼写里没有 r 的词，只要以 /ə/ 或 /ɔː/ 收尾，英音也会补一个 /r/：idea of → idea·r·of、law and order → law·r·and、I saw it → saw·r·it。语法上算"不规范"，但英音里极其普遍，听得出就行。

Why it matters

  • Listening: V→V linking is why "go away" sounds like one word "gowaway", and "I am" like "ayam". Recognising the glides unlocks a big chunk of fast speech.
  • The British accent itself: linking /r/ (and intrusive /r/) is the most recognisable marker of British English. Get this right and you sound distinctly BrE, not generic.

Check yourself （先判断 /w/ /j/ /r/，再点开听）

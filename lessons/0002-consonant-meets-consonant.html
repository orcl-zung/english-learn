Phase 1 · Speaking Lesson 0002 · ~8 min

Consonant meets Consonant （辅音 + 辅音：长、合、吞）

What happens when word 1 ends in a consonant and word 2 starts with one? You already met one case — "bu(t) Sam". Here's the whole system.

Today's win: When two consonants collide, you'll know in a heartbeat whether to hold, merge, or drop — and the phrase "next day" stops tripping your ear.

The idea （三种结局）

英语不在词与词之间停顿。当辅音撞上辅音，只会发生三件事：

  1. HOLD 长读 — 两个相同辅音 → 只发一次，拉长（gemination）。
  2. MERGE 融合 — 相近的辅音融为一体，最典型 /t/ /d/ + /j/ → /tʃ/ /dʒ/（yod-coalescence）。
  3. DROP 吞音 — 夹在两个辅音中间的 /t/ 或 /d/ 直接消失（elision）。

① HOLD — same consonant, said once, long （相同辅音长读）

When word 1 ends and word 2 begins with the same consonant, you don't say it twice. You hold it — one sustained sound bridges both words, with no release in between.

bad dog
bad dog  ·  /bæd dɒɡ/ → /bæ:dɒɡ/  两个 /d/ 黏成一个长 /d/

More to hear — listen for the length, not two separate sounds:

  • ten nights  — 一个长 /n/
  • black cat  — 一个长 /k/
  • good day  — 一个长 /d/

陷阱：如果你把它们读成两个分开的辅音（"bad…dog"），听起来就像一个字一个字蹦的外国腔。拉长它。

② MERGE — /t/ /d/ + /j/ fuse into /tʃ/ /dʒ/ （融合）

This is the famous one. When /t/ or /d/ meets a /j/ sound (the "y" in you, your, yet), they weld together:

/t/ + /j/ → /tʃ/ ("ch")   ·   /d/ + /j/ → /dʒ/ ("j")

don·choo
don't you  ·  /dəʊnt juː/ → /dəʊntʃuː/
go·tcha
got you  ·  /ɡɒt juː/ → /ɡɒtʃuː/  → 就是口语里的 gotcha

More:

  • would you → "wouldja" /wʊdʒu/
  • meet you → "meecha" /miːtʃu/
  • did you → "didja" /dɪdʒu/

③ DROP — elision (吞音) of /t/ and /d/ （夹心辅音消失）

The rule is simple: a /t/ or /d/ that sits between two other consonants often vanishes.

结构： 辅音 — t/d — 辅音  →  中间的 t/d 掉了

next day
next day  ·  /nekst deɪ/ → /neks deɪ/  /t/ 夹在 /s/ 和 /d/ 中间 → 掉
last time
last time  ·  /lɑːst taɪm/ → /lɑːs taɪm/

More:

  • old man  — /d/ 夹在 /l/ 和 /m/ 中间 → 掉
  • just one  — /t/ 掉
  • I don't know  — /t/ 掉 → "I-don-know"

注意：/t/、/d/ 在元音或 /h/ 前面不会吞（"not at all" 里 /t/ 还在，那是上一课的 C→V 连读）。只在夹心结构里掉。

英音小彩蛋 · glottal stop /ʔ/：英音里，词尾的 /t/ 在另一个辅音前常常变成"喉塞音"——气流在喉咙处顿一下，而不是清脆地吐出来。比如 water /wɔːʔə/、cat flap /kæʔ flæp/。这是英音的招牌，和上一课的 linking /r/ 一样有辨识度。先能听出来就行，不用急着练。

Why it matters

  • Listening — this is the big one. A lot of "I didn't catch that" is elision and merging. "What did you do?" sounds like "whaddaya do?". Once your own mouth produces these, your ear starts catching them.
  • Speaking fluency: IELTS Speaking rewards natural rhythm. Reading "don't you" as two separate words sounds robotic and caps your pronunciation score.

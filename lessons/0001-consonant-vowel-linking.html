Consonant → Vowel Linking （辅音 + 元音 连读）

The single highest-leverage habit for sounding natural in English — and the easiest one to feel.

Today's win: Take any phrase where word 1 ends in a consonant and word 2 starts with a vowel, and say it as one smooth sound — no gap in the middle.

The idea （一句话讲清）

中文是一个字一个字地念，词与词之间有小小的停顿。但英语不是 —— 英语会把词"粘"在一起。最常见的一种粘法就是：

前一个词以辅音结尾，后一个词以元音开头时，
那个辅音不要停，直接滑到下一个词的元音上。

It's as if the consonant at the end of word 1 moves over and becomes the first sound of word 2:

an apple
choppy: an · apple   (two blocks)
smooth: a-napple   /əˈnæpl/

The n didn't disappear — it jumped across to apple. Same mouth, same sounds, just no gap. That lack of a gap is the whole skill.

Why it matters

  • IELTS Speaking grades pronunciation partly on rhythm & flow. Word-by-word speech caps you around band 6; smooth linking signals band 7+.
  • Listening — a lot of "I didn't catch that" is actually "I didn't expect two words to sound like one." Once you produce links, you start hearing them.
  • It's the foundation: features 2–5 (consonant-consonant, vowel-vowel, weak forms, stress) all build on this idea of words flow into each other.

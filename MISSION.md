# Mission: English — IELTS 7.5+ & Beautiful Spoken/Written English

## Why
You already *read* English comfortably — articles, X/Twitter — on a solid high-school base. But you can't yet *produce* it: writing stalls at simple sentences, and speaking doesn't flow. The concrete goal is **IELTS 7.5+** (C1), which unlocks study, work and migration doors. Beneath the score, you want to **sound good** — to read sentences aloud with natural linking (连读), and to write without translating word-by-word from Chinese.

## Success looks like
- IELTS overall ≥ 7.5, with **Writing ≥ 7.0** and **Speaking ≥ 7.0** (the two scores that cap most Chinese candidates).
- Read a paragraph aloud and hear smooth **linking**, not word-by-word chop.
- Write a 250-word IELTS Task 2 essay with accurate tense, articles and subordinate clauses — without stopping to check grammar.
- Choose a preposition instinctively in common collocations (`in / on / at / of / with / for`).
- A sustainable daily habit that fits a 20-minute subway commute.

## Constraints
- **Time:** ~20 min/day on the subway (currently 3–4 Duolingo lessons) plus whatever you add at home. Lessons must stay short and commute-friendly.
- **Level:** B1–B2, "eating the old capital" of high-school English (≈120/150). Strong passive vocabulary & reading; weak active production.
- **Known weak spots:** no systematic grammar; **prepositions**; writing output; reading aloud with connected speech.
- **Strengths to build on:** good reading comprehension, a daily Duolingo streak, genuine motivation.

## Out of scope (for now)
- Heavy Listening & Reading test drilling — already your strength; revisit only at the IELTS-strategy phase.
- A second foreign language; accent coaching beyond "natural & clear"; bulk vocabulary-list memorisation.
